About Me

          
The engineer behind the work.

        


        

          

            

              I'm Naga Veera Venkata Satya Narasimha Murthy — Nagu — a B.Tech Cyber Security Engineering student who builds things with Python and works at the Robotics Lab.
            

            

              I don't think like a hacker first. I think like an engineer. Understanding how systems are correctly built is the prerequisite for understanding how they fail. That order matters — and it shapes every project I touch.
            

            

              Most of my best work happens between 11 PM and 3 AM. Not because of hustle culture — because it's quiet, focused, and the interruptions stop. I automate what repeats, I dig into what's hard, and I don't ship until I understand what I've built.

How I think

          
Six rules I don't break.

        

        

          

            01
            
Engineer first, hacker second.

            
Understanding correct systems is the prerequisite for understanding broken ones. I don't skip that step.

          

          

            02
            
Discipline is the strategy.

            
Motivation is noise. Systems, habits, and compounding effort are the actual method.

          

          

            03
            
Useful over impressive.

            
A tool that solves a real problem quietly is worth more than a demo that impresses a recruiter.

          

          

            04
            
Access implies responsibility.

            
In security, capability and ethics are inseparable. I treat them that way, regardless of who's watching.

          

          

            05
            
Automate the repeat work.

            
Doing anything twice is a signal. I listen to that signal and remove myself from the loop.

          

          

            06
            
Understand before claiming.

            
I don't list things I can't explain under questioning. Shallow breadth is a liability, not a skill.
